YOUR RIGHTS:

1. RIGHT TO COUNSEL
You have the right to be represented by an attorney. If you cannot afford an attorney, the court will appoint one to represent you free of charge. You may request a court-appointed attorney at your arraignment.

2. RIGHT TO REMAIN SILENT
You have the right to remain silent and not incriminate yourself. Anything you say can and will be used against you in a court of law.

3. RIGHT TO A SPEEDY TRIAL
You have the right to a speedy and public trial by an impartial jury. This means your case should be heard within a reasonable time period.

4. RIGHT TO CONFRONT WITNESSES
You have the right to confront and cross-examine any witnesses who testify against you.

5. RIGHT TO PRESENT EVIDENCE
You have the right to present evidence and call witnesses on your behalf.

6. RIGHT TO INTERPRETER
If you do not speak or understand English, you have the right to an interpreter at no cost to you.

FAILURE TO APPEAR:
If you fail to appear at the time and place specified, a warrant may be issued for your arrest, and you may be charged with an additional crime.

IMPORTANT:
This notice is not a determination of guilt. You are presumed innocent until proven guilty beyond a reasonable doubt.
